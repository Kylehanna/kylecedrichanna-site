About

Why work with me.

I build AI systems that survive an audit — an AI engineer and data scientist who turns sensitive, high-stakes data into private systems people can actually trust: traceable, logged, and defensible.

My work has been embedded with the White House and the University of Chicago, alongside more than 30 research and innovation offices. The demos on this site — Translation Radar, the Real-Estate Demand Radar — are real systems I've built. I find the foundational risks first, build systems your existing team can actually run, and design for governance from day one — so what you ship holds up when a client, a regulator, or opposing counsel asks how it works.

FAQ

Straight answers before you reach out.

How much does this cost?

The AI-Readiness Check is free. The AI-Readiness Review is a fixed fee, credited toward your build if you proceed. Builds and workshops are scoped to the work. Tell me what you're trying to fix and you'll get a straight number — no drawn-out sales process.

Why a Review before jumping into a build?

Most AI work fails because it's built on the wrong foundation — bad data, unclear ownership, or a use case that can't survive scrutiny. The Review finds the load-bearing problems first, so you don't spend real money on a build that won't hold up. It de-risks the work for both of us.

How fast do you move?

The Review is delivered in 1–2 weeks. Build timelines depend on scope, but you'll have a phased plan with milestones before any larger engagement begins.

Do we need technical staff on our side?

No. The systems are designed to be run by your existing team. Part of the Review is assessing exactly how much technical lift each option requires — and favoring ones you can operate without a data scientist on payroll.

Is our data kept private?

Yes. Your data stays yours. I work under NDA, don't train external models on your proprietary data, and deploy inside your own environment — security, logging, and traceability are built in, not bolted on.

What do we actually walk away with?

From the Review: a written exposure-and-compliance map, the 2–3 workflows worth automating first, and an architecture plus build proposal. Yours to keep, whether or not we build together.
